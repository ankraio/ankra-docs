---
title: Getting Started
description: Essential steps to set up and manage infrastructure efficiently with Ankra
---

We aim to make navigating the Ankra Platform a self-explanatory experience. If you're new, this quick start guide will walk you through the essential steps to set up and manage infrastructure efficiently.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Import Existing Cluster">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Import Cluster">
        For existing Kubernetes environments, importing any cluster allows for immediate visibility and management within Ankra.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Clusters</b>:</b> Access the Clusters section from the main dashboard to view and manage your infrastructure.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select <b>Import Cluster</b>:</b> Start the import process to bring your current Kubernetes cluster under Ankra’s management.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Define your initial infrastructure components:</b> Specify which workloads, namespaces, or resources you want to manage from the outset.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Run the Helm agent command:</b> Ankra provides a Helm command tailored for your cluster. Run this on your Kubernetes cluster (including local setups like Minikube or Kind) to establish a secure connection.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Manage infrastructure via Add-ons:</b> Once imported, you can maintain or expand your cluster’s capabilities by subscribing to and deploying add-ons directly from the Ankra UI.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Values files stored safely:</b> If you disconnect from Ankra, your configuration (values files) is preserved in a Git repository of your choice, ensuring you retain full control and auditability.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Connect to GitHub">
        Store and version-control your infrastructure configuration securely.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Integrations</b>:</b> Access the Integrations section to connect external services.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select <b>Connect GitHub</b>:</b> Authorize Ankra to access your GitHub account. This enables automated storage of values files and configuration history.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Choose your repository:</b> Pick the repository where Ankra will store your cluster’s configuration files, ensuring transparency and easy rollback.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Deploy Add-ons">
        Enhance your cluster with essential tools and services.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Add-ons</b>:</b> Browse a curated marketplace of add-ons, including monitoring, security, networking, and more.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Subscribe to add-ons:</b> Select the add-ons you need and subscribe to them for your cluster.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>One-click deployment:</b> Deploy add-ons instantly using default values, or customize them for your environment.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Create profiles for customization:</b> For advanced use cases, define profiles to standardize or customize add-on configurations across clusters or teams.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create Profiles">
        Standardize and reuse infrastructure patterns.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Go to <b>Add-ons &gt; Subscribed</b>:</b> View the add-ons you’ve enabled for your cluster.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Create a profile:</b> Click into an add-on, then go to Profiles and select Create. Profiles let you define reusable configuration templates.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Set default values:</b> Specify default or enforced values for the add-on, making it easy for others to deploy consistent infrastructure.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Save for future use:</b> Profiles can be reused by you, your team, or shared publicly for broader adoption.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Share Infrastructure Profiles">
        Promote best practices and consistency across teams.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Open <b>Profiles</b>:</b> Access all your saved profiles from the Profiles section.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select and share:</b> Choose a profile and use the Share option to grant access to individuals, teams, or the public.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Define permissions:</b> Control who can view, use, or modify the profile, ensuring secure and consistent usage.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Create New Cluster">
    <Note>
      Ankra provisions vanilla clusters with deeper automation than traditional EKS, providing more control and flexibility.
    </Note>
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create Cluster">
        Provision a new Kubernetes cluster from scratch with Ankra’s guided workflow.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Clusters</b>:</b> Start from the Clusters section in the dashboard.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select <b>Create Cluster</b>:</b> Choose to create a new cluster (currently AWS is supported).
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Configure settings:</b> Set your preferred region, cluster size, and Kubernetes version to match your workload needs.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Confirm and provision:</b> Review your settings and launch the cluster. Ankra automates the provisioning process, including networking and security best practices.
          </li>
        </ul>
        <Note>
          If you disconnect from Ankra, the cluster remains fully functional in your AWS account.
          Any Add-ons installed continue running as defined in the values files, which are stored in a repository of your choice.
        </Note>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create Credentials">
        Securely manage cloud provider access for deployments.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Go to <b>Credentials</b>:</b> Access the Credentials section to manage cloud access keys.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select provider and input details:</b> Choose your cloud provider (e.g., AWS) and enter the required credentials.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Save credentials:</b> Credentials are securely stored and can be reused for future deployments.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Connect to GitHub">
        Enable automated, version-controlled configuration management.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Integrations</b>:</b> Connect your GitHub account for storing values files.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Authorize access:</b> Grant Ankra permission to write to your chosen repository.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Select repository:</b> Choose where your cluster’s configuration will be stored for transparency and rollback.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Deploy Add-ons">
        Add essential capabilities to your new cluster.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Navigate to <b>Add-ons</b>:</b> Explore available add-ons for monitoring, security, networking, and more.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Subscribe and deploy:</b> Subscribe to the add-ons you need and deploy them with a single click.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Customize via profiles:</b> For advanced setups, create profiles to standardize configurations across environments.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create Profiles">
        Build reusable templates for infrastructure components.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Go to <b>Add-ons &gt; Subscribed</b>:</b> Manage your deployed add-ons.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Create a profile:</b> Define and save configuration templates for yourself, your team, or public use.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Set and enforce values:</b> Ensure consistency and compliance by setting default or required values.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Save for reuse:</b> Profiles streamline future deployments and promote best practices.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Share Infrastructure Profiles">
        Distribute standardized infrastructure patterns.
        <ul>
          <li>
            <b>Open <b>Profiles</b>:</b> Access your saved profiles.
          </li>
          <li>
            <b>Share with others:</b> Grant access to individuals, teams, or the public, and define permissions as needed.
          </li>
        </ul>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
